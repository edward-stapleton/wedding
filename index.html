Flying

If you’re travelling from overseas — thank you! London has several major airports with good connections to Oxford:

  • Heathrow Airport (LHR): Heathrow is the closest major airport to Oxford. The journey by car is around 70 minutes.
  • Gatwick Airport (LGW): From Gatwick, the Airline coach also offers a direct service to Oxford, running every hour and taking about 2 hours 15 minutes. You can also take a train from Gatwick to Reading, then change for a direct train to Oxford (around 2 hours total).
  • Luton Airport (LTN) and Stansted Airport (STN): Both airports are further away, but still manageable. The easiest route is to take a train or coach into London and then continue by train from either Paddington or Marylebone to Oxford. Total travel time is usually around 2.5–3 hours.

Train

Oxford is very easy to reach from London by train:

  • From London Paddington: Direct trains to Oxford run frequently (about every 15–30 minutes) with Great Western Railway (GWR). The journey takes around 50–70 minutes, depending on the service.
  • From London Marylebone: You can also travel with Chiltern Railways from London Marylebone. These trains take about 1 hour to 1 hour 15 minutes, arriving at Oxford or Oxford Parkway station.

Once in Oxford, the main station is just a short walk or taxi ride from the city centre and most hotels. Tickets can be booked in advance through the National Rail website or apps like Trainline for the best prices.

Car

If you prefer to drive, Oxford is about 65 miles north-west of London and is easily reached via the M40. The journey typically takes around an hour or two, depending on traffic. There is plenty of parking at the reception venue and some street parking near the church.

After the ceremony, our journey to The Medley Walled Garden will be part of the day’s experience; a short stroll across Port Meadow with beautiful riverside views and the odd cow or horse.

On foot (recommended)

The walk takes around 15 minutes at a leisurely pace. There's no need to worry about directions — we'll have local sherpas (Ed's friends) guiding the way, so everyone can relax and enjoy the scenery. Ladies — we recommend bringing a pair of comfortable flats to make the meadow stroll more enjoyable.

By taxi or car

If walking isn’t ideal, taxis or car lifts take around 5–10 minutes, and local taxi services can be booked in advance. Parking at the Medley Walled Garden is plentiful, and cars can be picked up the next day.
